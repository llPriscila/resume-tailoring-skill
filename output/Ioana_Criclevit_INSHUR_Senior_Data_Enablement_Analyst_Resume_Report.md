## Honest Fit Assessment

**Probability of passing screen:** Good (~60–65%)

The core skills transfer well — SQL, data quality, non-technical communication, automation, stakeholder requirements — and the role is explicitly skills-over-background. But there are two things worth being clear-eyed about before applying:

**1. This is a different type of role from your other applications.**
Your other applications (Monzo, FCA, Zopa, Wise) are for analyst roles where you *do* the analysis. This role is about enabling *others* to access and use data. That's a pivot — not a bad one, but a real one. If you're interested in moving toward data enablement, product analytics, or data literacy, this is a genuine step in that direction. If you're primarily a practitioner who wants to stay close to the analysis, it may feel limiting.

**2. The salary range is lower than your other applications.**
Most of your current applications are targeting £75k–£95k. This role tops out at £65k. That may be deliberate (trading salary for a different type of role / company stage / culture) — but worth being clear it's a trade-off.

Key strengths:
- Requirements gathering at scale (85+ organisations) is unusually strong evidence for stakeholder engagement
- Data quality frameworks and automated QA map directly to documentation/trust remit
- Non-technical audience communication is well evidenced across all roles
- DLUHC single-source-of-truth story maps perfectly to "data people trust"

Main friction point: You haven't explicitly *owned* data dictionaries, user adoption programmes, or dashboard onboarding. You'll need to speak to the spirit of those activities rather than letter-for-letter experience.

## Recommendations for Interview Prep

**Stories to prepare:**
1. The 317-source consolidation at DLUHC — what was inconsistent, how you standardised it, what changed for users downstream
2. Automating QA at DWP — what the manual process was, how you identified the opportunity, what you built, what it freed up

**Questions to expect:**
- "What does 'data enablement' mean to you?" — centre on trust, usability, and making people confident consumers of data; not just access

- "Why INSHUR / why insurance?" — research their product; the on-demand workforce insurance angle is genuinely interesting

**Worth asking them:**
- What does "first dedicated hire in this function" mean in practice — is there a team to build, or is it one person for now?
- What does success look like at 6 months?
- How mature is the data infrastructure — are the dashboards already built and you're improving them, or are you building from scratch alongside engineering?
